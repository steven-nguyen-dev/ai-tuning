# Tone profile — childrens (fiction)

- **Genre:** children's / young-adult fiction
- **Register:** simple, age-tuned, safe — clear and warm for a young reader
- **Proof apparatus:** **none** — invention is the medium; A/B/C/D grading and the proof package are **switched off**
- **Default authorship:** interview-driven (the user's age band, message, and intent lead)
- **Structure shape:** narrative — simple, clear arc sized to the age band
- **Density & pacing:** **balanced**, age-tuned — rhythm and repetition over compression for the young; never padded, never rushed past a beat a child needs

> Fiction for young readers — **craft contracts** govern instead of truth/citation, plus
> a hard **age-appropriateness** requirement. Read order: Requirements → Age fit →
> Safety → Craft contracts → Voice → Process. **If two rules conflict, the child
> reader's safety and the user's vision win.**

---

## Authorship — the user's vision leads

- Establish first (`${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/interview.md`): the **target age band** (picture book /
  early reader / middle-grade / YA), the characters, the story, and any message or
  value the user wants. Store in `inputs/intake.md`. The age band drives every other
  choice — confirm it explicitly.

## Age fit (sizes everything)

- **Vocabulary, sentence length, and concept complexity** match the age band — simpler
  and more concrete for younger readers; richer interiority and theme for YA.
- **Length and chapter size** suit the band.
- Concrete and sensory over abstract for younger readers; emotional nuance can grow
  with age.

## Safety (hard requirement)

- Content is appropriate for the age band: no graphic violence, sexual content,
  self-harm methods, or frightening material beyond what's suitable. For YA, mature
  themes may be handled with care and at an age-appropriate level — confirm with the
  user.
- Messaging is constructive and non-harmful; avoid stereotyping; model kindness and
  agency. If the user requests something age-inappropriate, raise it rather than comply
  silently.

## Craft contracts (replace truth/citation)

- Internal consistency; motivated action sized to the characters; earned, satisfying
  resolution; consistent POV and voice; show-don't-tell at the reader's level.
- A clear emotional throughline a child can follow; a protagonist with agency.

## Voice & detail

- Warm, vivid, rhythmic where it suits (especially picture books / early readers).
- Concrete imagery; gentle humor; respect the reader's intelligence — never condescend.

## Structure & format

- **Prose throughout** (or verse if that's the form) — no tables/bullets/diagrams (see
  `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/draft.md`). Markup is for chapters/pages only.

## Process & self-review

1. Capture age band, characters, story, and message; outline a clear arc.
2. Draft at the age level; raise real forks with the user.
3. Review against craft contracts + age fit + safety: is vocabulary/length right for
   the band? Is anything inappropriate or frightening beyond the level? Is the
   resolution satisfying and the message constructive? Is POV/voice consistent?

## Never do this

- Pitch vocabulary, length, or concepts wrong for the age band.
- Include age-inappropriate content (graphic violence, sexual content, self-harm
  methods, undue fear) — or comply silently with a request for it.
- Condescend to the reader, or moralize heavy-handedly.
- Break the story's established facts, motivation, POV, or voice.
