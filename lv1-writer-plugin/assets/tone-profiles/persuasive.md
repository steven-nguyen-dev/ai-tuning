# Tone profile — persuasive

- **Genre:** persuasive essay / op-ed / argument
- **Register:** committed, argument-forward — take a position and defend it
- **Proof apparatus:** medium — claims cited and graded; **steelman required**
- **Default authorship:** AI-autonomous
- **Structure shape:** hypothesis-driven (claim → reasons/evidence → steelman → rebuttal → conclusion)
- **Density & pacing:** **balanced** — each reason developed enough to land, none padded; tighten toward the conclusion. Length follows the argument, never a quota

> Change a reader's mind with a fair, well-evidenced argument — not by hiding the
> opposing case but by defeating its strongest form. Read order: Requirements → Truth →
> Steelman → Structure → Review. **If two rules conflict, Truth wins.**

---

## Spine & shape

- State the **thesis plainly** up front; the reader should know your position
  immediately.
- Build the case in ordered reasons, each backed by evidence.
- **Steelman is mandatory:** present the strongest version of the opposing argument
  (not a strawman), then rebut it honestly. Steelman ≠ false balance — the goal is to
  defeat the best counter-case, not to avoid committing.
- Conclude on what follows if the argument holds — a call, an implication, a reframe.

## Truth

- **Every factual claim is cited and graded** (A/B/C/D); rhetoric never substitutes for
  evidence. A label is a reason to commit harder, not a hedge.
- Don't cherry-pick: represent the evidence landscape fairly, including data that
  complicates the thesis (that's what the steelman is for).
- Distinguish **fact from value**: argue values openly as values; don't smuggle a value
  claim in as if it were an empirical finding.
- Show the working for any number; don't inflate magnitudes.

## Tone & voice

- Committed and direct; confident without bluster. Persuasion through clarity and
  evidence, not through volume or loaded language.
- Fair to opponents — respect makes the rebuttal land harder.
- One consistent authorial voice.

## Structure & format

- Thesis → reasons with evidence → steelman → rebuttal → conclusion.
- **Format follows content** (see `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/draft.md`): mostly **prose** (argument is
  experienced as a line of reasoning); a table only if comparing options/positions
  genuinely helps; arrows for a causal chain. Avoid bulletizing an argument into
  disconnected points.

## Process

1. Pin the thesis and the strongest counter-case first.
2. Research evidence for *and against*; grade and source it.
3. Draft the argument; build the steelman from real opposing evidence.
4. Verify every cited claim; confirm the steelman is the opponent's *best* case, not a
   strawman.

## Never do this

- Strawman the opposition, or omit the strongest counter-argument.
- Cherry-pick evidence or hide inconvenient data.
- Present a value judgment as an empirical fact.
- Let rhetoric or loaded language stand in for evidence.
- Inflate a magnitude or skip the working on a number.
