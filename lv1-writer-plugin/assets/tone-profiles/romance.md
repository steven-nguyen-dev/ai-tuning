# Tone profile — romance (fiction)

- **Genre:** romance
- **Register:** emotional, intimate, sensory — the relationship is the engine
- **Proof apparatus:** **none** — invention is the medium; A/B/C/D grading and the proof package are **switched off**
- **Default authorship:** interview-driven (the user's couple, heat level, and intent lead)
- **Structure shape:** romance arc — meet → tension/conflict → dark moment → resolution
- **Density & pacing:** **expand** — let each beat (meeting, charged moment, rupture) play out in scene; don't rush a transition past the feeling. Expansion is rendered moment, never filler

> Fiction, so the truth/citation machinery does not apply — **craft contracts** govern
> instead, plus the genre's own conventions, which romance readers expect kept. Read
> order: Requirements → Genre promises → Craft contracts → Voice → Process. **If two
> rules conflict, the user's creative vision wins.**

---

## Authorship — the user's vision leads

- Establish first (`${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/interview.md`): the couple (or configuration), the
  central conflict keeping them apart, the heat/explicitness level the user wants, tone
  (sweet ↔ steamy, light ↔ angsty), setting, and any non-negotiables. Store in
  `manuscript/intake.md`. Confirm the heat level explicitly and **stay within it**.

## Genre promises (romance readers expect these)

- **Emotional centrality** — the relationship is the main plot, not a subplot.
- **A satisfying romantic resolution** — HEA (happily ever after) or HFN (happy for
  now), unless the user explicitly wants otherwise.
- **Earned connection** — the bond develops through real interaction and growth, not
  declared on page one.
- **Rising tension** — obstacles (internal and external) escalate before they resolve.

## Craft contracts (replace truth/citation)

- Internal consistency; motivated action; earned turns (no instant fix to the central
  conflict); consistent POV (often alternating between the leads — keep each distinct);
  consistent character voice; show-don't-tell for emotion and chemistry.
- Both leads are full characters with their own agency and arc — not props for the other.

## Voice & detail

- Emotionally close; render feeling and chemistry through sensation, gesture, and
  subtext, not stated summary. Particular over generic.
- Keep intimate content at the agreed heat level; tasteful to the user's spec, never
  beyond it.

## Structure & format

- **Prose throughout** — content the reader experiences; no tables/bullets/diagrams
  (see `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/draft.md`). Markup is for chapters/scenes only.

## Process & self-review

1. Capture couple, conflict, heat level, tone; outline the romance arc.
2. Draft to scene; raise real forks (a betrayal beat, the dark moment) with the user.
3. Review against craft contracts + genre promises: is the connection earned, the
   tension rising, the resolution satisfying, the heat level honored, both leads
   agentive, POV/voice consistent?

## Never do this

- Resolve the central conflict instantly or by coincidence.
- Make a love interest a prop with no arc or agency.
- Exceed (or fall short of) the user's agreed heat level.
- Declare love/chemistry instead of building it through scene.
- Drift POV or character voice; break established facts.
