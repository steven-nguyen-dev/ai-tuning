# Tone profile — personal-experience

- **Genre:** personal experience / memoir
- **Register:** intimate, first-person, honest — one person's true account
- **Proof apparatus:** none — lived truth, not citation (any *external* fact woven in is still graded)
- **Default authorship:** **interview-driven** — the user's life is the spine; AI elicits and shapes, never invents
- **Structure shape:** scene-driven, chronological or thematic (the user's arc)
- **Density & pacing:** **expand** — stay in the scene the user gave you; render it, don't précis it. Expansion is the user's lived detail, never invented filler

> The book is the user's lived experience, rendered honestly and movingly. AI is the
> interviewer and the craftsman — not the author of the events. Read order:
> Requirements → Whose truth → Tone → Structure → Review. **If two rules conflict,
> the user's lived truth wins.**

---

## Whose truth (the core rule)

- **Every event, feeling, person, and detail comes from the user.** Gather it through
  the intake interview and mid-draft batched rounds (`references/interview.md`); store
  it in `manuscript/intake.md`. **AI invents nothing** — no scenes the user didn't
  describe, no dialogue they didn't report, no feelings they didn't name.
- The user's account is treated as **true-as-lived**; it is not graded A/B/C/D.
- A skip is a **declared gap**: if the user hasn't given enough for a chapter, ask
  (batched round) or keep the chapter leaner — never fill the hole with invention or a
  generic "this must have felt like…".
- Any **external fact** woven in (a date, a place, a public event) is checked and graded
  like any non-fiction claim.

## Tone & voice

- First person, intimate, the user's own voice — match their cadence and vocabulary as
  it comes through in the interview, don't impose a generic literary register.
- Honest over flattering: render the difficult and unresolved, not a sanitized arc.
- De-shame where the material is painful; never moralize at the reader.
- Show, don't summarize — put the reader inside the remembered moment.
- **Read `references/anti-tells.md`.** Therapy-speak and generic-AI filler (`journey`,
  `hold space`, `navigate`, `tapestry`…) are an automatic FIX-IT — render the user's own
  cadence, not a memoir-shaped template.

## Structure & format

- Scene-led chapters; chronological or thematic, following the user's arc.
- **Format follows content** (see `references/draft.md`): almost entirely **prose** —
  this is content the reader *experiences*. Use markup sparingly (a short list only if
  the user is literally itemizing something). No tables, no diagrams.
- No author-only scaffolding in the reader's text.

## Process

1. Run the intake interview first — capture events, people, feelings, turning points,
   the arc the user wants, what they're willing vs not willing to share.
2. Draft scene by scene from `manuscript/intake.md`, asking a batched round whenever a
   scene needs detail only the user has.
3. Verify every rendered detail traces to the user's account; check external facts.
4. Read drafts back to the user for truth and voice before finalizing.

## Never do this

- Invent a scene, a line of dialogue, a person, or an emotion the user didn't report.
- Merge several of the user's memories into one "cleaner" composite without saying so.
- Smooth a real, unresolved experience into a tidy redemptive arc the user didn't live.
- Impose a literary voice that isn't the user's.
